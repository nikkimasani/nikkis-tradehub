🧠 What Is Day Trading?
▸

Day trading means buying and selling financial instruments within the same trading day. The goal is to profit from short-term price movements.

  • Pattern Day Trader Rule: 4+ trades/5 days needs $25k PDT Rule
  • Most beginners start with paper trading Start Here
  • Risk management is more important than strategy Critical

🎯 Opening Range Breakout Strategy
▸
  • Wait for first 15–30 min candle to close
  • Mark the high and low of that range
  • Enter on breakout above (long) or below (short)
  • Stop below the range low (for longs)
  • Target 2x the range height minimum
  • Confirm with above-average volume

🧮 Position Size Calculator
The single most important tool for protecting your trading account. Never enter a trade without running this calculator first. Position sizing is how professional traders stay in the game long-term.
Why This Matters

Most beginner traders blow up their accounts not because their analysis is wrong, but because they risk too much on any single trade. A bad trade on 10% of your account is survivable. A bad trade on 50% can end your trading career. The calculator enforces mathematical discipline.

How to Use the Calculator — Step by Step
  1. Set your Account Size — Enter your total trading account balance. If paper trading, use your virtual amount (e.g. $10,000). This never needs to change unless your account grows or shrinks significantly.
  2. Set your Risk % — This is the percentage of your account you're willing to lose on this one trade. 1% is the standard for beginners. On a $10,000 account, 1% = $100 max loss per trade.
  3. Find your Entry Price — This is the price you plan to BUY the stock. Mark it on your chart BEFORE doing this calculation.
  4. Find your Stop Loss Price — This is the price where you'll exit if the trade goes against you. It should be below a key support level (for longs). Set this BEFORE you enter, not after.
  5. Set your Target Price (optional) — Where you plan to take profit. Used to calculate Risk:Reward ratio.
  6. Read the results — The calculator shows exactly how many shares to buy, how much of your account is at risk, and whether the trade meets your rules.
Understanding the Results
Result FieldWhat It MeansWhat to Look For
Dollar RiskThe actual dollar amount you'll lose if your stop is hitShould equal Account × Risk% (e.g. $100 on $10k at 1%)
Shares to BuyThe number of shares that makes your risk exactly equal to your dollar riskUse this exact number — don't round up
Total Position ValueTotal dollar amount of the trade (Shares × Entry)Should be a reasonable fraction of your account
% of Account UsedWhat percentage of your account is tied up in this positionKeep under 20% to stay diversified
Risk:Reward RatioHow many dollars you can make for each dollar riskedMinimum 1:2 — ideally 1:3 or better
Potential ProfitDollar profit if price hits your targetShould be at least 2× your dollar risk
The Formula (for reference)
Dollar Risk = Account Size × (Risk% ÷ 100)
Shares to Buy = Dollar Risk ÷ (Entry Price − Stop Price)
Risk:Reward = (Target − Entry) ÷ (Entry − Stop)

Example: $10,000 account, 1% risk, Entry $50, Stop $49, Target $52
Dollar Risk = $10,000 × 0.01 = $100
Shares = $100 ÷ ($50 − $49) = 100 shares
R:R = ($52 − $50) ÷ ($50 − $49) = 2.0 → "1 to 2"
Rule Checker

The right panel automatically checks your trade against three rules and displays color-coded feedback:

✅ Rule passes ⚠️ Borderline — proceed with caution ❌ Rule fails — do not take this trade
If the Rule Checker shows any ❌, do not take the trade. The most common failure: risk% over 2%, or R:R below 1:1. Skip any trade that doesn't meet your rules — there will always be another setup.
📓 P&L Journal
Your structured trade log and performance analytics center. Logging every trade — wins and losses — is the single most important habit you can build as a developing trader.
Why Keep a Trade Journal?

Without data, you can't improve. Traders who journal consistently can identify their best setups, their worst habits, and their most profitable times of day. Traders who don't journal keep making the same mistakes. After 50–100 logged trades, patterns will emerge that change how you trade.

Logging a Trade
  1. Date — Pre-filled with today's date. Change it if logging a trade from a different day (format: MM/DD/YYYY).
  2. Symbol — The stock ticker. Type it in uppercase (e.g. AAPL, NVDA).
  3. Direction — Long (you bought, expecting it to go up) or Short (you borrowed and sold, expecting it to go down).
  4. Entry ($) — The price you paid to enter the trade.
  5. Exit ($) — The price you received when you exited the trade.
  6. Shares — Number of shares traded. Use the Position Calculator to determine this before the trade.
  7. Setup — Optional. Name the pattern or setup you traded (e.g. "ORB", "VWAP bounce", "Bull flag").
  8. Notes — Optional but important. Write the lesson learned or what you noticed (e.g. "Entered too early before confirmation", "Should have held longer").
  9. Click + Log Trade. P&L is calculated automatically.
Understanding the Analytics Bar
MetricWhat It MeansTarget for Beginners
Total P&LSum of all trade profits and losses. Green = net profitable, red = net loss.Focus on process, not this number, for the first 50 trades
Win RatePercentage of trades that were profitable (P&L > $0)50–60% is respectable. Don't chase 90%+ — it means you're not letting winners run.
Trades LoggedTotal number of trades in the logNeed at least 30–50 trades for stats to be meaningful
Profit FactorTotal gross profit ÷ total gross loss. A PF above 1.5 means the strategy makes money long-term.PF > 1.5 is good. PF > 2.0 is excellent. PF < 1.0 means losing money overall.
A 40% win rate with a 1:3 R:R ratio is MORE profitable than a 70% win rate with a 1:0.5 R:R ratio. Win rate alone means nothing. Profit Factor and average winner vs. average loser tell the real story.
Deleting a Trade

Click the × button on the right side of any trade row. A confirmation prompt will appear. Once deleted, the trade is removed and all analytics recalculate immediately.

Sort your trade log by setup name to see which setups are most profitable. If "ORB" trades are consistently green and "VWAP bounce" trades are red, stop trading VWAP bounces and double down on ORB.

News moves markets. Here's how to use the feed as part of your pre-market routine:

🎯 Paper Trade 90-Day Challenge
A structured 90-day beginner challenge to build trading discipline, track simulated performance, and develop consistent habits before risking real money.
What is the 90-Day Challenge?

Paper trading means practicing with fake money while using real market conditions. The 90-day challenge gives this practice structure: you commit to trading for 90 consecutive trading days, logging your results, and tracking whether you followed your rules each day. After 90 days, you'll have real data on your performance and know whether you're ready for live trading.

Professional trading firms require new traders to paper trade for 3–6 months with consistent profitability before they're allowed to trade firm capital. 90 days is the minimum credible baseline.
Starting the Challenge
  1. Click 🚀 Start 90-Day Challenge. Today becomes Day 1.
  2. Set up a paper trading account at Webull or thinkorswim (both free) with a virtual balance matching your intended real account size (commonly $10,000–$25,000).
  3. Trade each day using your dashboard: use the Charts tab for analysis, Position Calculator for sizing, and News tab for context.
  4. After each trading session, return to the Paper Trade tab and log your day.
Logging a Daily Check-In
  1. Paper P&L ($) — Enter your profit or loss for the day from your paper trading platform. Use negative numbers for losses (e.g. -80).
  2. Trades Taken — How many trades did you make today? Enter 0 if you stayed out of the market.
  3. Notes — Write one sentence about what you learned or observed today.
  4. Rules Checklist — Check each box honestly. Did you follow all 5 rules today?
  5. Click Log Day.
The 5 Daily Rules
RuleWhy It Matters
Only traded defined setupsRandom trades are gambling. Defined setups build a repeatable edge over time.
Used a stop-loss on every tradeNo stop = undefined risk. One unprotected trade can wipe out weeks of gains.
Risked no more than 1–2% per tradeMathematical survival. Never let one trade put your account in serious danger.
Stopped after 3 losing tradesRevenge trading after losses causes most account blowups. Walk away and come back tomorrow.
Did pre-market research before tradingPreparation separates professional traders from gamblers. Know your watchlist before the open.
Reading the Calendar

The 90-day calendar shows one dot per day. Each dot has a meaning:

Win day — P&L was positive Loss day — P&L was negative Rules day — No trades taken, but rules followed Empty — Day passed, not logged

Today's dot has a gold border. Hover over any past dot to see the date. The dot also shows the P&L amount directly if it fits.

Streak Counter

The streak counter tracks how many consecutive days you checked all 5 rules as followed. This is a discipline score, not a P&L score. A trader with 60/90 days of full rule compliance will almost always outperform a trader with 10/90 — regardless of whether individual days were profitable.

A "rules day" (zero trades, all boxes checked) is better than a loss day. Knowing when NOT to trade is as valuable as knowing when to trade. The best traders sit out when conditions are unclear.
When Are You Ready for Live Trading?

Consider going live when all of the following are true after your 90-day challenge:

  • ✅ Profitable or breakeven over the full 90 days (not just the last 2 weeks)
  • ✅ Followed all 5 rules on at least 70 of 90 days
  • ✅ Profit factor above 1.3 in your P&L journal
  • ✅ You understand WHY every winning and losing trade happened
  • ✅ You can clearly describe your 2–3 setups without hesitation
If you're not consistently profitable in paper trading, you will not be profitable in live trading. The only difference is that losses become real. Do not rush this step.

🔬 RSI Simulator
An interactive, real-time visualization of how RSI (Relative Strength Index) works. Adjust parameters and see how RSI responds to different price movements.
What is RSI?

RSI (Relative Strength Index) is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes. It ranges from 0 to 100 and is used to identify overbought and oversold conditions.

RSI = 100 − (100 ÷ (1 + RS))
RS = Average Gain over N periods ÷ Average Loss over N periods
Default N = 14 periods
Using the Simulator Controls
ControlWhat It Does
RSI PeriodThe number of candles used to calculate RSI. Lower = more reactive/noisy. Higher = smoother/slower. Standard is 14.
Overbought LevelThe RSI level above which a stock is considered "overbought" (potential reversal down). Standard is 70.
Oversold LevelThe RSI level below which a stock is considered "oversold" (potential reversal up). Standard is 30.
VolatilityHow volatile the simulated price movement is. Higher volatility = bigger RSI swings.
How to Read RSI
RSI > 70 — Overbought (look for reversal signals) RSI < 30 — Oversold (look for bounce signals) RSI 40–60 — Neutral / ranging RSI divergence — price makes new high but RSI doesn't = warning
RSI overbought/oversold signals alone are not entry signals. A stock can stay overbought for a long time in a strong trend. Always combine RSI with price action and volume before acting on a signal.
🎓 Classroom
A built-in video player that pulls your YouTube playlists directly into the dashboard. Browse and play trading education videos without leaving your workspace.
Setting Up the Classroom

The Classroom requires a free YouTube Data API v3 key to fetch your playlist videos. Here's how to get one:

  1. Go to console.cloud.google.com and sign in with a Google account.
  2. Click New Project, give it any name (e.g. "TradeLab"), and click Create.
  3. In the left menu, go to APIs & Services → Library. Search for YouTube Data API v3 and click Enable.
  4. Go to APIs & Services → Credentials. Click Create Credentials → API Key.
  5. Copy the generated key. In the Classroom tab, paste it into the YouTube API Key field and press Save.
  6. The playlist video lists will load automatically. Click any video to play it in the built-in player.
The YouTube API key is stored in your browser's localStorage — it's never sent anywhere except Google's API. The free tier gives you 10,000 units/day, which is plenty for personal use.
Adding Your Own Playlists

The Classroom is pre-loaded with two curated trading playlists. To swap them for your own:

  1. Open any YouTube playlist you want. The URL will look like: youtube.com/playlist?list=PLxxxxxxxx
  2. Copy the playlist ID (the part after list=).
  3. Open dashboard.html in a text editor. Search for CLS_PLAYLISTS and update the playlist IDs and default names.

💡 Pro Tips for Beginners
Lessons that most traders learn the hard and expensive way. Read these before you trade a single dollar — real or paper.
Daily Routine That Works
  1. Pre-market (8:30–9:15 AM) — Open TradeLab. Check Live News tab for overnight news. Run Finviz screener for gappers. Build your watchlist for the day (2–3 stocks max).
  2. Pre-open (9:15–9:30 AM) — Mark key levels on your chart (support, resistance, pre-market high/low). Know your entry, stop, and target for each setup BEFORE the bell.
  3. First 15 minutes (9:30–9:45 AM) — Observe. Do not trade. Let the open volatility settle. Watch which direction the market wants to go.
  4. Prime time (9:45 AM–11:30 AM) — Highest-quality setups and liquidity. Best time to trade ORB and momentum plays.
  5. Midday (11:30 AM–2:00 PM) — Avoid. Volume dries up, spreads widen, moves are choppy and unreliable.
  6. Afternoon (2:00–4:00 PM) — Second-best window. Volume picks back up. Watch for trend continuation or reversals into the close.
  7. After close — Log your trades in the P&L Journal. Check all 5 rules in the Paper Trade tab. Write one sentence about what you learned.
Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid
MistakeWhy It HurtsThe Fix
Trading without a stop-lossOne unprotected trade can destroy weeks of gainsSet your stop before you enter, not after
Averaging down on losing tradesDoubles your risk on a trade that's already wrongExit when your stop is hit. No exceptions.
Chasing breakouts after they happenYou buy the top and become the exit liquidityWait for a pullback to a key level for entry
OvertradingCommission costs and poor setups compound lossesSet a max of 3 trades per day until consistently profitable
Moving your stop loss"I'll just give it more room" leads to large lossesYour stop is your plan. Stick to it.
Revenge trading after a lossEmotional decisions after losses lead to larger lossesMax 3 red trades = close the platform and walk away
Trading every daySome days have no setups — trading anyway is gamblingNo setup = no trade. Cash is a position.
The Trader's Mindset

Trading is a probability game, not a certainty game. Even the best setup fails 40% of the time. Your goal is not to be right on every trade — it's to make more money on your winners than you lose on your losers. A losing trade that respects your stop is a GOOD trade. A winning trade that violated your rules is a BAD trade. Judge your performance by process, not outcome.

The traders who make money long-term are not the ones who are right the most often. They're the ones who cut losses quickly and let winners run. Discipline beats intelligence in trading.

How to Make Changes & Redeploy

The entire dashboard lives in a single file: C:\Users\nikki\trading-dashboard\dashboard.html

  1. Edit the file — open dashboard.html in VS Code or any text editor and make your changes.
  2. Test locally first — double-click the file to open it in Chrome and verify your changes look right before deploying.
  3. Open PowerShell and navigate to the folder: cd C:\Users\nikki\trading-dashboard
  4. Deploy: vercel --prod
  5. Done — same URL, updated in ~10 seconds. No prompts after the first deploy.
You do NOT need to reinstall Node.js or the Vercel CLI again. Just open PowerShell, navigate to the folder, and run vercel --prod. That's it every time.

  • 📁 Keep a backup — before big changes, duplicate the file: dashboard_backup_YYYYMMDD.html
  • 🔍 Use Find in VS Code — Ctrl+F to search the file. Ctrl+G to jump to a line number.

  4. Wait 5–60 minutes for DNS to propagate. Your custom URL is live.
